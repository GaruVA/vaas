# Chapter 10 — Conclusions

VAAS demonstrates that a computer-vision-based vehicle attendance system, built specifically for Colombo Dockyard PLC's gate and shift environment, can address the two concurrent failure modes of the existing UHF RFID system — electromagnetic interference from the adjacent SLPA port authority network and behavioural card-scanning fraud — in a single deployable solution. The system achieves 91.3% end-to-end plate recognition accuracy across CDL's four operational lighting conditions and processes gate events at a 95th-percentile latency of 294 ms, sustaining a 41% margin against the 500 ms operational target.

The CDL Specialisation Layer elevates the system beyond a generic attendance logger: drydock zone topology, vessel-project attribution, and subcontractor company management are native first-class entities in the data model, enabling the personal-vehicle fuel allowance report, OHS compliance classification, and subcontractor billing audit that CDL's Finance, HSE, and Project Management departments require. The SHA-256 hash chain provides mathematically tamper-evident records suitable for payroll dispute resolution without requiring external cryptographic infrastructure.

The 160-test non-YOLO test suite, with zero failures, and the Waitress-served Flask application sustaining eight concurrent dashboard sessions confirm that the system is production-ready in its current form for CDL's single-site deployment profile. The identified gap — production latency validation on the Jetson Nano target platform — is the primary prerequisite for physical gate installation.

Future work should prioritise a driver self-service portal, production hardware benchmarking, and dataset expansion to cover degraded plates, in that order. A multi-site topology migration to a client-server database engine is the medium-term architectural evolution required if CDL's subsidiary facilities are to be brought under the same attendance and analytics platform.
